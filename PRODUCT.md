# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults (25–55) in the local area considering a private dentist for themselves or their family. They arrive via word of mouth, social media, or search. They're comparing Denlux against other private clinics — not NHS — and are already willing to pay a premium; they're evaluating trust and quality, not price. On the site they want to quickly answer: "Is this place credible, what do they do, and how do I book?"

## Product Purpose

A public-facing marketing website for Denlux Dental (by H&S), led by Dr. Saad Ahmed. The site exists to convert interested visitors into booked appointments by demonstrating clinical excellence wrapped in genuine premium presentation. Success means a prospective patient finishing the page confident enough to call or book.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Warm. Considered.

The clinic feels like a private members' venue — dark moody interior, warm lighting, leather — but the brand voice is approachable and human, not cold or corporate. The cyan accent is the only loud note; everything else is restrained. The brand should feel like a clinic that takes craft seriously without being intimidating.

## Anti-references

- SaaS health-tech aesthetic: dashboard energy, hero metric callouts, gradient cards, bold stat badges. Denlux is not a startup.
- Overcrowded local clinic sites: low-budget WordPress with exhaustive service lists, stock photography, no hierarchy, every section the same visual weight.
- Generic NHS / insurance dental: teal-on-white, badge-heavy trust signals, clinical form-factor with no personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Confident restraint** — whitespace is an editorial choice, not empty space. Let each section breathe. The silence between elements signals quality.
2. **The doctor is the brand** — Dr. Saad Ahmed is a person, not a service. Humanise the practice through his presence and craft, not a list of qualifications.
3. **Precision over abundance** — fewer services shown with more care beats a complete list shown poorly. Curate; don't catalogue.
4. **Warmth earns trust** — the aesthetic is quiet luxury, but the copy and layout should feel approachable. Expensive but not cold.
5. **The cyan accent earns its place** — one energetic note in an otherwise restrained palette. Use it with intention on primary CTAs and key moments, nowhere else.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. 4.5:1 minimum contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations. No motion-gated content reveals.
